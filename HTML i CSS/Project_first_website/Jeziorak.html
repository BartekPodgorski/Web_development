Jeziorak

Jeziorak (formerly Gizerek, Jezierzyce, German Geserich See) - a gutter lake located in north-eastern Poland within the Iława Lake District. Jeziorak is the longest and sixth largest lake in Poland in terms of surface area. Within it, there is the Water Volunteer Ambulance Service in Iława. In 1304, in Jeziorak documents, it is mentioned under the name Geyserich, and in 1346 - Geyzeri. The name derives from the Prussian language from the word geeys - heron. The original name would mean 'Lake Czaple'. One of the islands of the lake has retained the name Czaplak (German: Czaplakwerde). On Jeziorak there are many bays, peninsulas and 16 islands. The largest are Ostrów (Great Żuława) and Wielki Bukowiec. The lake is connected to the Elbląg Canal. The banks of the lake are forested - the northern high stand, and the southern low. The town of Iława is located on Jeziorak.
